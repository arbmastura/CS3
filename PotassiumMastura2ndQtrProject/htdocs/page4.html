Plant Tissues.

TYPES OF PLANT TISSUES

Ground Tissue

The most common and dominant tissue in plants, it heals wounds and replaces tissue, such as in traumas, diseases, or infections. It is a photosynthetic layer (mesophyll); an area for gas exchange; and serves for food storage in starch.

Dermal Tissue

The skin or outermost layer of a plant. Its epidermis is one cell thick and has no chloroplasts; contains a waxy cuticle evolved from Cambrian Explosion for water retention and protection.

Vascular

Present in vascular species. Not found in nonvascular species such as moss.

MERISTEMS

Apical Maristems

Lengthens and causes primary growth at tips of shoots and roots, producing flowers and leaves.

Lateral Meristems

Widens and causes secondary growth at carnbium, causing tree barks.

SIMPLE PERMANENT TISSUES

Parenchyma

Has thin cell walls with intracellular air spaces and packages and stores tissue for gaseous exchange and osmosis.

Collenchyma

Is unevenly thickened but with thick corners, supports with flexible walls and photosynthesizes.

Schlerenchyma

Has evenly thick walls and functions in strength, such as in barks; with schlereid observed in nuts and stone fruit.

COMPLEX PERMANENT TISSUES

Xylem

Dead and empty; elongated cells; large lumen; cell walls with lignin; and pits for lateral water movement. Transports water and minerals in a one-way direction.

Vessels are round and end-to-end; tracheids have spindles and overlap.

Phloem

End-to-end with sieve plates and companion cells, transporting sugars in two-way direction.

Animal Tissues.

EPITHELIAL TISSUES

Squamous

Thin and irregularly shaped with large, flattened nucleus, and found in mouth, alveoli, and skin surface.

Columnar

Elongated cells with oval nucleus with or without cilia, secretes mucus and absorbed digested food, as in intestines or gallbladder.

Cuboidal

Square shaped with spherical nucleus; absorbs and secretes, as in the sweat gland, thyroid, and kidneys

NERVE TISSUE

Sensory Neurone

Senses signals

Relay neurone (interneuron)

Relays information

Motor neurone

Commands muscles for reaction

An example involving the three neurones includes reflexively withdrawing one's hand upon touching a hot object.

CONNECTIVE TISSUE

Areolar

Binds epithelium in matrix (or cytoplasm for nucelus) of elastic collagen and fibers, found between skin and organs.

Fibrous

A dense network of elongated rows of non-elastic collagen, called fibroblasts, in tendons and ligaments.

Cartilage

Chondrocytes found in matrix; absorbs shock, as in joints, ears, and nose.

Blood

Consists of Lymphocytes, Erythrocytes, and Platelets; transporting, waste, nutrients, and hormones, in the circulatory system.

Adipose

Fat cells, for energy storage and insulation, found under skin and around organs

Bone

Network of systems, osteocyte in solid matter. Structures body and muscle attachment in the skeletal system.

MUSCLE TISSUE

Skeletal Muscle

Striated with multiple nuclei, voluntary, and attached to bones.

Smooth Muscle

Not striated with thin spindle fibers; involuntary, found in digestive system and blood vessels.

Cardiac Muscle

Faintly striated; network of branched muscle fibers; involuntary; found in heart.
